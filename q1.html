Question 1 (Part A)

Differentiate the roles and tasks involved in curriculum and courseware design and development.

Guiding Points for Response

Difference between Curriculum & Instruction

  • Curriculum (The "What" & "Why"): High-level architecture. Involves establishing objectives, parameters, and the overall structure. Focuses on "what" needs to be learned and "why".
  • Instruction (The "How"): Detailed design and development. Involves selecting specific teaching strategies, media, and materials. Focuses on "how" the learning will be delivered.

Tasks Distinction

  • Curriculum Tasks: Needs analysis, defining learning outcomes, determining assessment strategy, high-level sequencing.
  • Instructional Tasks: Storyboarding, creating slides/videos, writing assessment items, facilitating the class.

Example: Customer Service Training Project

Role Specific Task in Scenario
Curriculum Designer Determined that "De-escalation Skills" is a core competency gap based on complaint data analysis. Decided the module should be 4 hours long.
Courseware Developer Wrote the specific role-play scripts for the "Angry Caller" scenario and designed the checklist used by observers.
