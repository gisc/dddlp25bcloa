Question 6 (Part A)

With reference to the section 4, analyse how content is packaged and sequenced to optimise learning.

Guiding Points for Response

Packaging Content

Deciding "how much" goes into a unit. Criteria (Ornstein & Hunkins):

  • Self-sufficiency: Does it stand alone?
  • Significance & Utility: Is it useful for work?
  • Learnability & Feasibility: Can it be learnt in the time available?

Sequencing Strategies (Smith, Stanley & Shores)

  • Simple-to-Complex: Foundations first.
  • Prerequisite Learning (Part-to-Whole): Bits before the big picture.
  • Whole-to-Part: Overview first, then details.
  • Chronological: Time-based / Process flow.

Example: Packaging & Sequencing

Strategy Application in Scenario
Packaging (Utility) Grouped all "System Navigation" skills into a pre-work eLearning module. This ensured face-to-face time was reserved for high-value "Soft Skills" practice which requires human interaction.
Sequencing (Simple-to-Complex) 1. Simple: Standard Greetings & Closings.
2. Intermediate: Information Giving (answering FAQs).
3. Complex: Objection Handling & De-escalation (Emotional regulation).
Rationale: Builds confidence before tackling stressful situations.
