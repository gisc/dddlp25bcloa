Guiding Points for Response

Constructive Alignment (Biggs)

The "Golden Thread" connecting:

  1. Learning Outcomes: What learners should be able to do (Cognitive/Affective/Psychomotor).
  2. Learning Activities: Activities that require learners to engage the outcomes.
  3. Assessment: Tasks that verify if the outcomes were met.

6 Principles of Learning Design

  • Authenticity
  • Alignment
  • Holistic
  • Future-Oriented
  • Feedback
  • Judgement

Example: Alignment & Principles

Element Application
Constructive Alignment LO: "Handle a customer objection."
Activity: Pair role-play with objection cards.
Assessment: Observation checklist during final simulation.
(Not a multiple-choice quiz on "what is an objection").
Principle: Authenticity Used actual recorded calls (redacted) from the previous month as case studies rather than textbook examples.
Principle: Feedback Implemented "Stop-Start" coaching during practice, where the facilitator pauses the role-play to give immediate correction, rather than waiting until the end.
