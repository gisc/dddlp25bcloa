Guiding Points for Response

Curriculum Parameters

Parameters are the constraints and requirements that shape your design decisions.

  • Purpose: Why does this curriculum exist? (Skill gap vs Compliance vs New Product).
  • Competencies & Skills: What capabilities must learners demonstrate? (Technical, Soft Skills, Behavioral).
  • Subject Matter: Is it Knowledge (cognitive), Skills (psychomotor), or Attitude (affective)?
  • Learner Profile: Experience level, literacy, location, demographics, prior knowledge.
  • Qualification / Job Level: Entry-level vs Advanced, Certification requirements, Industry standards.

Impact on Curriculum Design

  • Entry Requirements: Prerequisites needed, prior experience, educational background.
  • Assessment Methods: Written test vs Practical performance vs Portfolio vs Observation.
  • Delivery Pathways: Classroom vs Workplace vs Tech-enabled (Online, Mobile, VR).
  • Delivery Methods: Lectures, Workshops, Coaching, Simulation, Self-paced modules.
  • Sequencing of Learning Units: Prerequisite structure, complexity progression, logical flow.

Example: Call Centre Customer Service Training - Parameter Influence

Curriculum Parameter Analysis Impact on Curriculum Design
Purpose Address high customer complaint rates and improve first-call resolution metrics. Business need: Reduce average handling time while maintaining customer satisfaction. Entry Requirements: Must include basic computer literacy and communication skills assessment. Assessment Methods: Focus on practical call handling metrics rather than theoretical knowledge tests.
Competencies & Skills Active listening, empathy, problem-solving, CRM system navigation, product knowledge, de-escalation techniques, compliance with data protection regulations. Delivery Methods: Combination of simulation-based training, role-playing exercises, and system hands-on practice. Assessment Methods: Live call observations, customer satisfaction scores, and practical CRM demonstrations.
Subject Matter Mix of cognitive (product knowledge, policies), psychomotor (system navigation), and affective (customer empathy, stress management) domains. Delivery Pathways: Blend of classroom instruction (policies), tech-enabled simulations (system practice), and workplace coaching (real call handling). Sequencing: Start with cognitive foundation, then psychomotor skills, finally affective integration.
Learner Profile Gen Z agents (18-25), high mobile usage, short attention span, mixed educational backgrounds, some with retail/customer service experience, tech-comfortable but need structured guidance. Delivery Pathways: Mobile-accessible microlearning modules, gamified learning platform. Delivery Methods: Short 10-15 minute learning bursts, interactive videos, peer learning sessions. Sequencing: Bite-sized units with immediate application opportunities.
Qualification / Job Level Entry-level customer service representative, industry certification required for handling financial products, compliance training mandatory for GDPR and financial regulations. Entry Requirements: High school diploma minimum, background check required, must complete compliance certification before handling live calls. Assessment Methods: Formal certification exams for compliance modules, recorded call quality assessments against industry standards.

Contextualise to your scenario. Each parameter creates specific constraints and opportunities that shape how you design effective learning experiences.
